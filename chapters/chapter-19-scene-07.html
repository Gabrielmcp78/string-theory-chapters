VII. For All
Largo eterno

As the latch catches, a new sound rises from the catacombs beneath.

A single tuning fork struck by no one is answered by a tone just higher, then another, and another.

The frequencies climb through the harmonic series, each overtone joining the next until the air itself becomes choir.

Low fundamentals murmur beneath bright, glasslike upper voices; a ladder of resonance built from matter and memory.

Then one—the sixth in the series—bends, just shy of true.

A blue note to cradle the whole world inside its imperfection.

The priest doesn’t know the melody, but the words are already on his lips, the way a name returns when you least expect it, tickling the back of your mind until you speak the name aloud.

He whispers them once,

and the question that has followed him since boyhood—

Why?—finally releases its hold.

Outside, the wind carries the harmony across the fingered hills.

  Inside, the chapel hums to keep the echo close.

The earth spins. Time sifts.

The Sun, radiant in its cosmic patience,   

yearning for a new song to sing—

waits… for whoever dares to hear what comes

   next.

On Equations, Interpretation, and the

Permission to Look Again

This novel makes deliberate use of real, established equations from physics and mathematics. These equations are not fictional inventions, nor are they presented as discoveries arising from the events described in the narrative. They predate the story and remain, in the real world, objects of active study, debate, and refinement.

What is fictional is not the mathematics itself, but the interpretive and structural role the narrative assigns to those equations under extreme conditions.

The novel advances hypotheses rather than declarations. Where those hypotheses intersect with empirical science, they remain open to confirmation, refinement, redirection, or rejection. None of those outcomes diminishes the coherence of the story. They are part of the same long conversation science has always had with itself.

A Note to the Scientific Reader

To scientists, physicists, and to anyone who has dared to take the universe seriously enough to try to understand it: this work is written in admiration.

I am not one of you. I do not claim your training, your rigor, or your precision. I have spent my life watching your work from the outside, reading it, struggling with it, revering it, and returning to it again and again with the same mixture of awe and frustration that marks any honest encounter with something larger than oneself.

This book is not an indictment of science. It is a love letter to the attempt.

If, at times, its implications press hard against the habits or boundaries of the profession, that pressure comes from respect, not disdain. The scientific dream has always been a daring one: to believe that reality is intelligible, that patterns matter, and that human curiosity is not misplaced in asking questions of the cosmos.

In writing this story, I mean to honor that daring.

I also mean to ask, gently but sincerely, whether there may still be room to look again at ideas dismissed too quickly, patterns set aside too early, or questions avoided because of how they resemble past mistakes. Not to abandon rigor, but to remember that rigor was once a rebellion against certainty, not a defense of it.

If this narrative overreaches, let it be corrected. If it errs, let it be shown how. That is the contract science has always made with itself.

But if, in some small way, it encourages renewed wonder, risk-taking curiosity, or generosity toward unconventional questions, then it has done exactly what it hoped to do.

Closing Note

Because, as the book states many times, this is not the end of the map.

There is so very much more.

More structure than we have named. More questions than we have permitted ourselves to ask. More room for rigor that does not harden into fear, and for imagination that does not drift into belief.

Science has never been finished. It has only ever paused, regrouped, and continued. Each generation inherits both its discoveries and its blind spots, and progress has always depended on the courage to notice the difference.

This appendix exists in that interval. It does not insist it is right.

It insists only that curiosity should not be disqualified by habit, and that exploration should not be mistaken for regression simply because it uses familiar numbers, old symbols, or unfashionable questions.

If there are doors we cannot yet open, there is still value in standing before them and knocking—listening.

So this is not a conclusion. It is an encouragement to continue.
