II. Wander and Wonder
Andante con gratitudine

So many things look different. New buildings. Taller trees.

Yet so much remains the same.

He is struck most by the students. The way they carry themselves. The way they move through space.

He begins to wander, without destination—for he has long since released the pressures that come with needing to get places.

A squirrel scampers across his path.

He stumbles just slightly—moving out of its way.

Two undergraduates leap up from a bench to help the old man regain his footing.

They are kind and generous of spirit, and he’s grateful—not necessarily for the help.

He is not yet that old.

No, he is glad for the interaction, glad for the hands-on proof that goodness still resides.

One of them has an Introduction to Quantum Harmonics textbook slipping from her knapsack, with a foreword by Dr. Marcus McGrath—President Emeritus of the SolPhi Institute of Universology.

“Quantum Harmonics?” He asks. “How are you enjoying the course?”

The student gleams with enthusiasm. “My absolute favorite class. Im mesmerized!”

“Keep on.” He says. “Theres so very much more.”

His head is calm.

His heart is full.

He’s glad already that he made the trip.

But he has wandered now, in this elation state, long enough to lose his bearings.

A young man approaches. Perhaps he will help.

The student notices, without a word, that the elder seems “loosely directed.”

The old man asks if he might be guided to the music school chapel.

Though it’s just around the corner, the student offers to accompany him, as the hill is slick with rain and the way somewhat treacherous.

He lifts his right leg, showing a freshly grass and mud-stained knee, imparting that he’s just come from there.

They walk together.

The company is a blessing.

Again, the man’s heart is filled with the kindness he finds.

Before even a moment has passed, they are standing in front of the entrance to the chapel on the hill.

The old man’s hands begin to shake.

He is nervous now.

But he tells the young student he’ll be fine, and thanks him.

A deep breath… then another, for good measure.

And the old man moves to enter the now ancient sanctuary.
